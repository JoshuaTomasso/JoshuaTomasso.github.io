Overview

Lizard Wizard is a top-down roguelite where the player explores a map to find and defeat three mini-bosses, then returns to spawn to activate the exit portal. My primary contribution was the sound system — building the audio architecture in C#, wiring sound triggers to gameplay events, and ensuring audio responses stayed clean and maintainable as the project grew.

Audio work is often undervalued, but it directly impacts player feedback, game readability, and the overall feel of moment-to-moment play.

  • Audio system design and implementation in Unity using C#
  • Gameplay event-driven sound triggering
  • Boss music transition system tied to trigger zones
  • Enemy idle audio by monster type
  • Player feedback and game feel through audio
  • Sound organization and maintainability

What I Worked On

Sound Manager

I built a singleton audio manager that centralizes all sound and music playback for the game, using Unity's AudioSource components to manage different types of audio across SFX, music, and menu channels.

  • Enum + SoundList — An enum categorizes 23 different sound effects and music tracks, each mapped to an index in the soundList array for easy lookup. The SoundList struct holds multiple audio clips per sound type, enabling random selection during playback. Adding more entries to the enum is supported, keeping the system expandable.
  • Sound Playback — PlaySoundClip(SoundType, volume) plays a random SFX clip through the SFX source. PlayEnemySoundClip(SoundType, source, volume) routes audio through a specific AudioSource for enemy-specific sounds. Dedicated methods handle menu hover and respawn effects.
  • Music Management — PlayBackgroundMusic and PlayBossMusic are coroutines that queue music with a short delay before playing. PlayMenuMusic plays menu music on loop. Each channel has its own stop, pause, and unpause methods so they operate independently.
  • Editor Support — An OnEnable() hook automatically resizes the soundList array in the editor and names each entry to match the SoundType enum, making inspector setup faster and less error-prone.

Centralizing all audio through a singleton manager means any script in the project can trigger a sound with a single function call — no AudioSource setup required at the call site. The enum-driven structure also makes it easy to add new sounds without restructuring existing code.

Boss Music

I built a trigger-based audio controller that manages the transition between background music and boss battle music as the player enters and exits a boss arena.

  • OnTriggerEnter() — When the player enters the boss trigger zone, background music pauses. If boss music isn't already playing, a new coroutine starts it and sets the playing flag. If it was already playing (e.g. the player re-enters after leaving), it unpauses from where it left off instead of restarting.
  • OnTriggerExit() — When the player exits the boss zone, boss music is paused (preserving playback position), background music resumes from where it left off, and the flag resets.

Pausing rather than stopping both tracks means neither resets when the player crosses the trigger boundary. Re-entering the boss room resumes the fight music seamlessly — a small detail that makes the audio feel intentional rather than jarring.

Audio Listener Positioning

I set up the audio listener to follow the player through the level using a dedicated empty object on the player prefab and a lightweight tracking script.

  • The audio listener is attached to an empty that also carries the AudioPositionChange script and a FaceCamera script.
  • Start — If no head position reference is assigned, the script finds the tagged HeadPos object in the scene automatically.
  • Update — Every frame, the listener's transform is set to match the HeadPos object's world position, keeping it locked to the player's location.

In a top-down game, keeping the audio listener synced to the player ensures that 3D spatial audio (positional falloff, stereo panning) behaves correctly as the player moves. Without this, sounds would be calculated from a fixed world position and feel disconnected from movement.

Enemy Idle Audio

I implemented per-type idle sound behavior on enemy prefabs so each enemy variety plays its own ambient audio on a repeating timer.

  • A MonsterType enum (EYE, WOLF, YETI) is set directly on each enemy prefab in the inspector, along with an idleSoundTimer float.
  • Update — If the enemy isn't currently making noise, the IdleNoise coroutine is started.
  • IdleNoise Coroutine — Sets the making-noise flag to true, waits for the idleSoundTimer duration, then switches on MonsterType to call PlayEnemySoundClip with the appropriate sound type and the enemy's own AudioSource. Resets the flag afterward.
  • Enemy AudioSources are configured with logarithmic rolloff (min distance 3, max distance 50) for realistic spatial falloff.

Routing idle sounds through the enemy's own AudioSource rather than a central emitter means each enemy produces sound from its actual world position, giving the player real spatial cues about where enemies are even when they're off-screen.

Notes

Gameplay Overview

Lizard Wizard is a top-down roguelite. The player explores the map to find three mini-bosses, each located in their own scene accessible through portals on the main map. After defeating all three, the player returns to spawn and activates the exit portal to finish the run. The main menu offers four options: Play, Settings (to configure options before starting), Credits (showing the team and their roles), and Quit.

Audio Architecture

The sound system is built around a central SoundManager singleton that persists across scenes and owns three AudioSource channels: SFX, Music, and Menu. All sounds are defined in a SoundType enum with 23 entries, each mapped to a SoundList struct that holds one or more audio clips for that type. Any script in the project plays a sound by calling into the manager's static instance — no local AudioSource setup required at the call site.

  • Clips are stored in serialized SoundList structs on the manager, auto-populated in the editor via OnEnable()
  • Sounds are triggered from gameplay scripts via PlaySoundClip or PlayEnemySoundClip, and from trigger zones for boss music
  • Background and boss music run as coroutines with a 0.25s startup delay; each can be paused and resumed independently
  • Enemy idle sounds play through per-enemy AudioSources with 3D spatial settings and logarithmic rolloff
